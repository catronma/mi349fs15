Smoking Other Things

Cheese

Smoking cheese gives it a "nut type", smokey flavor unlike a fresh cheese. Because cheese can begin melting at temperatures above 90 degrees, you will need to use a cold smoke method. Cold smokers are available for purchase for this specific purpose, but it can also be accomplished with some plans found on the internet as a more inexpensive route.

Eggs

Take a dozen eggs and put them onto one rack in smoker. Smoke them at 225-235 degrees for at least two hours. They go into the smoker uncooked because their shells are permeable enough to allow the smoke to get into egg white.

The real key is a steady temperature and avoid spikes in temperature.
